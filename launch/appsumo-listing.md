## The Problem (the section that converts cold AppSumo browsers)

### You're sending blind.

Most email marketers check one number before they hit send: **bounce rate**. That's like checking tire pressure before a road trip and ignoring the engine.

Your list is constantly broadcasting **7 signals** that determine whether your next campaign hits the inbox or spam. Most tools only measure one. InbXr reads all 7 and gives you a single score so you know exactly where you stand.

### What InbXr tells you that nothing else does:

- ✅ **Real human engagement after Apple Mail Privacy Protection** (most tools fake this — InbXr uses User-Agent + Apple IP + timing detection, not just iCloud domain matching)
- ✅ **Microsoft DMARC 2025 enforcement compliance** (May 2025 cutoff is live — `p=none` no longer works)
- ✅ **Acquisition cohort quality** (find out which signup channel is dragging your list down)
- ✅ **Dormancy risk by 180-day and 365-day cohorts** (the contacts that become spam complaints)
- ✅ **Authenticated SPF + DKIM + DMARC + List-Unsubscribe** scored against the 2024-2025 ISP requirements
- ✅ **Trajectory analysis** — is your Signal Score climbing or in free-fall?

## What's inside InbXr

### The Signal Score Engine
Connect your ESP (Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, Mailgun, AWeber) or upload a CSV. Get a Signal Score out of 100 with a letter grade (A through F), broken down by all 7 signals, in under 60 seconds.

### Signal Watch (automatic monitoring)
Every 6 hours, InbXr re-pulls your list, recalculates the 7 signals, and fires alerts if anything trends toward danger. No more finding out from your unsubscribe rate.

### Early Warning alerts
8 pre-built alert rules covering bounce spikes, engagement drops, authentication failures, dormancy buildup, blocklist hits, and more. Trend-based language only — no fake "your list will die in 23 days" predictions.

### Signal Rules (with dry-run preview)
Build your own automation rules: "if dormant for 365+ days, suppress" or "if bounce rate > 5%, alert." Every rule defaults to **dry-run mode** so you can preview the affected contacts before going live.

### Recovery Sequences
When a segment goes cold, InbXr generates a 2-3 email re-engagement sequence using Groq (Llama 3.3 70B) tuned to your brand voice. Export to Mailchimp drafts in one click.

### Send Readiness gate
A composite green/amber/red traffic light before every send. Pulls all 7 signals in real time and tells you whether to proceed, watch, or hold.

### Signal Map
Visualize your contacts by segment — Active, Warm, At-Risk, Dormant — with provider distribution, MPP density, and bounce flags overlaid. Find the cohort dragging your list down at a glance.

### Signal Advisor (the AI consultant, not a chatbot)
Built on Groq with full per-user signal context injected into the system prompt. Ask "why did my Signal Score drop?" and get a real diagnosis based on your actual numbers, not generic advice.

## FAQ

**Is this lifetime or subscription?**
Lifetime. One payment, you own it.

**What ESPs do you support?**
Per-contact integrations: Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, Mailgun, AWeber. Aggregate-only (Signal Score still works, with reduced fidelity): Instantly, Smartlead, GoHighLevel. CSV upload supported for any ESP not on the list.

**Can I use this without an ESP integration?**
Yes. CSV upload gives you 5 of the 7 signals immediately. The 2 engagement-dependent signals require either an ESP connection or you can run InbXr against the same list weekly to start building trajectory data.

**What happens if I exceed my contact cap?**
Your existing data stays. New contacts beyond the cap won't sync until you upgrade or remove old ones. No surprise bills.

**How is this different from ZeroBounce or NeverBounce?**
Those are list verification tools — they check if addresses are valid. InbXr does that *and* reads 6 other signals about your list health, sender reputation, engagement quality, and trajectory. Verification is one signal of seven.

**How is this different from GlockApps or Mail-Tester?**
Those run seed list deliverability tests on individual campaigns. InbXr looks at your *list itself*, before you've drafted the next send. They're complementary — InbXr tells you if you should send, seed tests tell you how it performed.

**Will my ESP keys be safe?**
Yes. All API keys are encrypted at rest with Fernet (cryptography lib) keyed off the application secret. We never store plaintext credentials.

**Do you have an affiliate program?**
